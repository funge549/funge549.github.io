soy sauce beef

This dish was created once I moved to New York City for college. The first ingredients I bought were soy sauce and sesame oil, as they are staples for Cantonese meals. By using combining these sauces and meat, we get this savoury and fragrant protein based meal.
Although pork and chicken are more often eaten in Chinese households, in Costa Rica and the USA beef is a lot more common choice of protein.

steamed napa cabbage

This dish is always present in the dining table, whether it is at my home, 爺爺奶奶家 & 婆婆公公家 (grandparent's house) or going out to eat to our usual weekend Chinese restaurant.
As a kid I wouldn't eat it, the texture wasn't the greatest and that it wasn't appetizing. But now if I don't eat these veggies with my meals I feel that the meal isn't complete. Napa cabbage is my favourite chinese vegetables as it is the perfect mix of soft leaves and crunchy stem.

jasmine white rice

When i was younger, I was a very picky eater and if there wasn't any dishes that suited my childish taste buds, I will only eat bowls of white rice [yes, just plain rice]. Thankfully, white rice was always an option in any restaurant or house in Costa Rica. I'm that person that needs to have rice at least once a day, and if I don't, it's okay. But my body will start to crave for it.
Steamed white jasmine rice is the perfect base to any Cantonese meal. It's the bridge betwen savoury, spicy, sour flavours from all meals. Nowadays, I'm also eating rice with mixed grains and beans it; a good way to have extra nutrition in it too!

HK style coffee

Most people know about Hong Kong style milk tea, but there is a tastier local favourite,
鴛鴦【yuenyeun】. This drink is 3 parts coffee and 7 parts milk tea. It's the perfect combination of bitter coffee and sweetened milk. The best way to drink this is to place the drink's glass in an ice bath, allowing the drink to be chilled but avoiding watering it down. Enjoy it during 'coffee time' as a energizer drink or as a weekend treat after a long week.

sweet tooth crocante

In any school party or gathering, someone will always offer to bring crocantes and those will be the first to be gone, no matter what. At first, it seemed too hard to create these snacks, but after making them a couple of times it is one of the easiest recipes of this collection.
The crunchy cereal mixed with the delightfulness of the dulce de leche, creates a perfect bite everytime. It’s a quick snack to make and it will suit to almost everyone’s taste.

lomo salteado

cuando me mudé a Nueva York para comenzar mis estudios universitarios, comencé a cocinar mucho y este es uno de los primeros platillos que cociné. ya que es una forma fácil y segura de cocinar la carne de res, con ingredientes que ya tenía en la casa. aunque el cerdo y el pollo son más frecuentes en comidas chinas y costarricences, la carne de res es mucho más accesible y preferida por los estadounidenses. por ende, este platillo es la combinación de carne de res con sabores únicos a la cultura cantonés.

mostaza china al wok

los vegetales al wok, son una constante en la mesa, sea mi casa o la de 爺爺奶奶 & 婆婆公公 (mis abuelos), o inclusive en el restaurante Chino, siempre van a haber vegetales. de niña nunca comía los vegetales, no me gustaba la textura de ella y no me sabía a nada gustoso. pero ahora, si no como vegetales con mi almuerzo o cena, siento que algo falta. la mostaza china es una de mis vegetales favoritos, ya que es una gran combinación de suaves hojas y crujientes tallos.
